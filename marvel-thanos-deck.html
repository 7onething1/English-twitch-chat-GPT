Kid mode: the Thanos team

This is a team for a card game. You and a friend play cards to win. Here is the easy version.

🟣 Boss cards, we call them bombs: Thanos is your strongest card. Get him out, keep him safe, and you usually win. Kingpin and Captain America are strong helpers.

🗡️ "No thank you" cards, we call them removal: Hour of Defeat and Repulsor Blast knock out the other player's monsters. You have four of them.

💰 Coin makers: Stark Industries Executive makes shiny Treasure coins that pay for any card, so your three colors work together.

🏝️ Lands are your energy. Play one every turn. You have 17, so you almost always have energy.

How you win: stay alive, clear their monsters, play Thanos, guard him, and fly over them with Thor and Human Torch.

Clean and honest: every land makes a color you actually play. Surveillance Room taps for any of your three colors, and the rest are basics — 6 Swamp, 6 Mountain, 4 Plains. Black, the removal base behind Hour of Defeat, rides on Swamps, Surveillance Room, and the three Executive Treasures. Red leans on the 6 Mountain plus Stark Industries Executive for the turn-one play, and white is a four-Plains splash that comfortably casts Captain America's double white.

No more dead duals: the old base ran three tapped lands (Avengers Hangar, Birnin Zana Plaza, Stark Industries) that only made a color a basic already covers, and just a single Plains. Cutting them for four Plains plus the one any-color land removes the tapped-land clog and makes the {W}{W} on Captain America a real cast instead of a prayer. The three Stark Industries Executive Treasures still smooth the three-color load.

Game plan

Survive, fix, dominate. Trade early with Hour of Defeat and HULK SMASH, ramp a Treasure off an Executive, land a bomb, and protect it. Thanos is a 4/4 deathtouch lifelink that trades up on turn three and sweeps the board late. Thor and Human Torch close from the air.

Mulligan hands with one color of mana or no early play. With four Plains, a wild Surveillance Room, and three Executives, keep two-color sevens that can reach the splash.

Sideboard notes

Why no green splash: the set's green bombs (Hercules, Prince of Power; Storm, Windrider; Hulk, Gamma Goliath; Moon Girl and Devil Dinosaur) tempt a fourth color, but even this fixing cannot support it.

Power swap held in reserve: if a game goes long, Madame Masque and Evil's Thrall are the flex slots. The pool's grade-30 leftovers stay in the box.
